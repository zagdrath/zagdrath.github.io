Controller Area Networks


by Cody L. Wellman

Introduction

Controller Area Networks (CAN) are a robust commnucations standard that was designed to allow microcontrollers and devices to communicate with each other's applications without a host computer. It is a message-based protocol and it was designed for multiplex electircal wiring in order to save on copper, but it can be used in many other contexts. The data in a frame is transmitted sequentially but in such a way that if more than one device transmits at the same time, the highest priority device can continue while the others back off. Frames are received by all devices, including by the transmitting device. CAN is commonly used in automotive, agricultural, robotics, and embedded control applications.
